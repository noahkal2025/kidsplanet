Programs at Kids Planet

Our Programs: Cambridge Preschool, Nursery, and Daycare

At Kids Planet Uganda, our programs are designed for young learners in Kampala who need a nurturing, supportive, and inclusive school. We offer baby school Kampala, nursery school in Kawempe, preschool in Kampala, and a flexible daycare centre in Kampala.

Whether your child is building early language skills, exploring the Cambridge curriculum, or enjoying early intervention support, our programs focus on every child’s growth.

Our learning community is rooted in Christian values and small class sizes, making us a trusted choice for families looking for the best nursery school in Kampala.

Program Options

Baby School

Gentle learning for children as young as one year old. We focus on routines, sensory play, and early social skills in a calm, caring environment.

Nursery School in Kawempe

Our nursery school prepares children for preschool with hands-on activities, early literacy, and plenty of opportunity to play and discover.

Preschool in Kampala

A Cambridge-based early years curriculum that introduces children to structured learning through play, stories, and guided practice.

Daycare Centre in Kampala

Safe daytime care that combines rest, play, and learning for busy families in Kampala.

Cambridge Curriculum with a Local Heart

Our Cambridge curriculum is child-centered and age-appropriate. It allows children to experience the joy of learning while building strong foundations in literacy, numeracy, and social skills.

We blend international standards with local values, so your child learns in a way that is both globally informed and deeply rooted in our Kampala community.

The result is a preschool program that supports academic readiness, emotional maturity, and a love for learning that lasts beyond the classroom.

Program Benefits

  • Structured learning with creative play.
  • Small class sizes for personalized attention.
  • Inclusive classrooms where every child belongs.
  • Christian values that encourage kindness and respect.
  • Support for children with special learning needs.
